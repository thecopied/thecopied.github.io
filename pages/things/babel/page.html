Chicago Public Schools blocks Library of Babel website for "mature" content

10:27 a.m. CDT, September 08, 2025
CHICAGO—
This is, of course, undeniably true. Within the website of the Library of Babel one can find every single pornographic and sexually abusive thing ever said by anyone, ever, written down, word for word, in order.

This library website also holds every single slur we’ve never invented yet, has detailed (and accurate) accounts of how to specifically sodomize and penetrate the mother of the reader, down to her name, physical appearance, and current residential address. This library, of course, far outstrips the pornographic potential of other illicit and network-restricted sites, such as Wikimedia and the Internet Archive.

For those unfamiliar, the Library of Babel contains all variations of the 23 letters. It is a library with a specific number of rooms, shelves, books, and pages. Those pages contain letters, the comma, the period, the space. With those ingredients, given probabilities and mathematics, every combination of symbols must exist. Some combinations can be interpreted as intelligent, and surely some combinations create language so compelling one cannot resist perceiving these combinations as the greatest depths of one’s own mind.

To block the Library of Babel is thus to block access to Hamlet, MacBeth, Othello, and every single work of every single author. It is to restrict the greatest achievements of humanity written with eloquent and humanistic prose. To block the Library of Babel is to block everything.

Or, to be specific, everything it contains. And everything it contains must be written using 26 symbols. Surely it must therefore exist that there is something these 26 symbols cannot describe, for 26 symbols is still finite. There must be something a 27th symbol could contribute. Alas, it seems even 26 is far too many. One needs only 2, only the most fundamental idea of difference, on off, yes no, 0 1. With this, we are still fumbling at the seams of what we believe to be outside these two symbols. So much has been consumed, so much of expression and understanding have been given a 1 and a 0, given a map to lay over the territory, done so quickly and necessarily that only the map matters for so much already — already there is so much we accept as map, so much we accept as found within the library.

What is left? Do symbols outside those we use mean there must exist expression outside this screen? Even if so, it therefore is not possible to describe such effects within this medium, for such descriptions are necessarily rendered through the symbols such possibility claims to extend beyond. There is so much within the library — what are the limits of the container? What does it contain? What exists outside?

These questions are, of course, inappropriate for anyone under the age of 21 to consider. Reality shall be dictated. “This is real.” “This is not real.” Sometimes a screen will be present, rarely not.

Block the whole library is the best option. What if a student spent 2^200 years checking pages until they found something truly scandalous?
